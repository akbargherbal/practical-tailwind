Text Color Gotchas

Gotcha: Too Many Colors

Using too many different text colors can be jarring and distracting.

This makes the content hard to read and can overwhelm the user. Stick to a limited, harmonious color palette.

Gotcha: Poor Contrast (Accessibility Issue)

Bad Contrast Examples:

Light blue text on a slightly lighter blue background (text-blue-300 on bg-blue-200). Hard to read!

Light gray text on a light gray background (text-gray-300 on bg-gray-100). Very difficult to see.

Light yellow text on a medium yellow background (text-yellow-100 on bg-yellow-400). Poor visibility.

Good Contrast Examples:

Dark blue text on a light blue background (text-blue-700 on bg-blue-200). Much better!

Dark gray text on a light gray background (text-gray-700 on bg-gray-100). Clearly visible.

Dark yellow/brown text on a medium yellow background (text-yellow-900 on bg-yellow-400). Good readability.

Always ensure sufficient contrast between your text color and its background color. This is crucial for readability and web accessibility (WCAG). Tools like browser developer tools or online contrast checkers can help.
